SVA.edu 4.0 is a website redesign for the School of Visual Arts, an art and design college in New York City. As part of the schoolâ€™s in-house design studio, Visual Arts Press, I worked with Gail Anderson, Eric Corriel, Brian Smith, Declan Van Welie, Ryan Durinick, Sam Potts, and Stephanie Gaito. I was the only full-time designer working exclusively on this project.

There were several facets to my involvement with redesigning sva.edu. We needed a custom CMS built from from scratch. When creating CMS features we often had lengthy debates over their UX. For implementing features, I had to create a prototype to demonstrate it's functionality. We used four different prototyping programs throughout the project: InVision, Framer, Principle, and Sketch's built-in prototyping tool. The prototypes were then sent to our developers, which I communicated with each day via GitLab.

Front-end redesign of sva.edu was done with Sketch, a program that I became very proficient in. In weekly design meetings with seven designers, I was in charge of implementing the design choices from the meetings. Thanks to my detailed knowledge of the project, I was responsible for maintaining design consistency across the frontend of sva.edu. I also made and maintained a huge Sketch library for all of our frontend design elements, and each element had to be made four times for each of our breakpoints.

Throughout the project and more so once the design was finished, I performed QA testing to ensure the functionality and design were executed to our specifications. Any issues I saw were my responsibility to catalogue and report to our developers.
